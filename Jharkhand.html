Tourist Places in Jharkhand

Deoghar

Deoghar is known for the Baidyanath Temple, one of the 12 Jyotirlingas, and is a major pilgrimage center in India.

Jamshedpur

Jamshedpur, known as the 'Steel City', is home to Tata Steel and beautiful parks like Jubilee Park and Tata Zoo.

Ranchi

Ranchi, the capital city, is known for its waterfalls, such as Dassam Falls and Hundru Falls, and scenic beauty.

Betla National Park

Betla National Park is a wildlife sanctuary known for its rich biodiversity, including tigers, elephants, and leopards.

Ghatshila

Ghatshila is a scenic town on the banks of the Subarnarekha River, known for its hills and waterfalls.

Shikharji

Shikharji is a major Jain pilgrimage site located on Parasnath Hill, the highest peak in Jharkhand.

Dasham Falls

Dasham Falls is a majestic waterfall located near Ranchi, offering picturesque views and trekking opportunities.

Netarhat

Netarhat, often referred to as the 'Queen of Chotanagpur', is known for its stunning sunrise and sunset points.

Massanjore

Massanjore Dam, built on the Mayurakshi River, is a picturesque spot ideal for boating and nature lovers.

Hazaribagh

Hazaribagh is known for its wildlife sanctuary and beautiful landscapes, making it a great destination for nature lovers.

Bokaro

Bokaro is famous for its steel plant, lush green parks, and the picturesque Garga Dam, a perfect spot for picnics.

Dhanbad

Dhanbad, known as the 'Coal Capital of India', is home to several coal mines and the famous Indian School of Mines.
