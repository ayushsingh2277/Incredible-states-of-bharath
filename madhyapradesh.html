Tourist Places in Madhya Pradesh

Khajuraho

Famous for its stunning temples with intricate sculptures, Khajuraho is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of India's rich cultural heritage.

Sanchi

Sanchi is renowned for its ancient stupas, particularly the Great Stupa, which are key symbols of Buddhist art and architecture.

Pachmarhi

Pachmarhi, also known as the 'Queen of Satpura', is a beautiful hill station with scenic landscapes, waterfalls, and rich flora and fauna.

Kanha National Park

Kanha National Park is one of India’s most well-preserved tiger reserves, offering a rich experience of wildlife and biodiversity.

Bhimbetka

The Bhimbetka rock shelters are an archaeological site with prehistoric paintings and carvings that date back thousands of years.

Bhopal

The capital city of Madhya Pradesh, Bhopal, is known for its beautiful lakes, historical monuments, and vibrant culture.

Ujjain

Ujjain is an ancient city famous for the Mahakaleshwar Temple and its connection to the Kumbh Mela, one of the largest religious gatherings in the world.

Omkareshwar

Omkareshwar is one of the holiest sites in India, located on the banks of the Narmada River, and is a major pilgrimage site dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Jabalpur

Jabalpur is known for its natural beauty, including the stunning Marble Rocks, Dhuandhar Falls, and its historical significance.

Orchha

Orchha is a historic town known for its magnificent palaces, temples, and the picturesque Betwa River that flows through it.

Gwalior

Gwalior is famous for its majestic Gwalior Fort, stunning temples, and the Scindia dynasty’s rich history.

Indore

Indore is a vibrant city, known for its food, historical sites, and its status as a major industrial and educational hub of Madhya Pradesh.
