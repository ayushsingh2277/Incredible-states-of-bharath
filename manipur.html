Tourist Places in Manipur

Loktak Lake

Loktak Lake, the largest freshwater lake in Northeast India, is famous for its phumdis - floating islands - and offers scenic views and boating experiences.

Tharon Cave

Tharon Cave is an ancient natural limestone cave offering a thrilling experience for adventure seekers and history enthusiasts.

Kangla Fort

Kangla Fort, located in Imphal, is an ancient and culturally significant site symbolizing Manipur's history and royal heritage.

Singda Dam

Singda Dam is the highest mud dam in the world, offering beautiful views and a tranquil atmosphere perfect for picnics and day trips.

INA Memorial Complex

The INA Memorial Complex commemorates the efforts of the Indian National Army during World War II, with exhibits and statues honoring fallen soldiers.

Shree Govindajee Temple

Shree Govindajee Temple, an important Vaishnavite temple, is a historical and spiritual center adorned with beautiful architecture.

Manipur Zoological Garden

Manipur Zoological Garden is home to several exotic animals, including the rare Sangai deer, Manipur's state animal.

Manipur State Museum

The Manipur State Museum displays artifacts and exhibits showcasing Manipur's rich history, art, and cultural heritage.
